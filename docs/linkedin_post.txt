============================================================
BELIEFBENCH: A PROBABILISTIC MICROSCOPE FOR FRONTIER MODELS
============================================================

Does an AI model merely choose the right answer - or does it
express an accurate, calibrated, stable and actionable belief?

BeliefBench evaluates an LLM against an exact Bayesian posterior
generated from a user-defined probabilistic world.

Latest GPT-4.1-mini financial study:

  [92.1%] Belief fidelity (1 - mean reference-view JS)
  [ 5.3%] Expected calibration error
  [0.0054] Repeated-call JS instability
  [70.0%] Agreement with the exact-posterior action
  [57.5%] Actions coherent with the model's own stated belief

That last result is the point.

The model's probabilities can be useful even when its direct actions
need deterministic downstream governance. Ordinary accuracy metrics
do not reveal that distinction.

BeliefBench measures:

  + Elicited belief accuracy
  + Behaviorally inferred beliefs
  + Calibration and repeatability
  + Robustness to missing and contradictory evidence
  + Decision consistency and regret
  + Balanced uncertainty and entropy strata
  + Exact information-set mismatch
